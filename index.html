About me

Hi there! I'm Amina Lawal, a DevOps Engineer and community leader passionate about transforming technology landscapes and empowering women in tech. With a background in Software Engineering and a focus on Cloud Infrastructure, I bridge the gap between complex technical solutions and meaningful community impact.

As a DevOps Engineer at HostSpace Cloud Solutions, I implement comprehensive DevOps solutions including CI/CD pipelines, monitoring systems, container orchestration, and infrastructure automation. My expertise spans the full DevOps lifecycle - from infrastructure as code and configuration management to observability and performance optimization, ensuring robust and scalable deployments for our clients.

Beyond my technical work, I'm deeply committed to advancing opportunities for women in technology. As the founder of G3Women and a Google Women Techmakers Ambassador, I've led diverse technical training initiatives that have impacted hundreds of women across Nigeria, spanning DevOps, Data Science, Web Development, and UI/UX design. My work with the Global Shapers Community, an initiative of the World Economic Forum, has created broader social impact through projects ranging from digital skills training, donation drives to healthcare awareness campaigns.

My journey combines technical excellence with community leadership - whether I'm optimizing cloud infrastructure, mentoring aspiring technologists, or driving initiatives that create lasting social impact. I believe in creating solutions that not only solve technical challenges but also create pathways for the next generation of leaders.
